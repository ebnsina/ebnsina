This is the part of the internet where I think out loud about software.

Engineering blogs tend to fall into two camps: either the surface-level tutorial
("how to center a div in 2026") or the impenetrable systems paper. I want to
write the thing in between &mdash; honest notes from the inside of real projects.

## What you'll find here

- **Post-mortems.** Bugs that taught me something. The fix is rarely the lesson.
- **Patterns that held up.** Architectures I'd recommend after running them in
  production for more than a quarter.
- **Tooling.** Small notes on the editor, the shell, the deploy pipeline.
- **Opinions.** Defended with code, not vibes.

## What you won't

- Hot takes on framework wars.
- AI doom or AI hype. I use it; I'll write about how, not whether.
- "10 things every senior engineer must know."

## Why a blog, in 2026

Because the half-life of a tweet is hours and the half-life of a good blog
post is years. I still find myself searching for posts written in 2014 about
PostgreSQL locking. That's the bar.

> If a post here saves one engineer one production incident, it's earned
> its hosting bill many times over.
